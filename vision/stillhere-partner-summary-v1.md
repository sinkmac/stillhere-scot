STILL HERE — PARTNER SUMMARY
One page · April 2026

The problem

In fifty years, your family will know your grandmother existed. They will find her name on a record and her dates on a certificate.

What they will not find is how she laughed, what she feared, what the world looked like when she was young, or what it felt like to be her.

This is not a failure of love. It is a failure of infrastructure.

What Still Here does

Still Here is a free public platform that gives ordinary people the tools to understand and record the lives of those who came before them.

The entry point is a single web tool. A user enters their name, their birth year, an ancestor's name and birth year. In seconds they receive an epoch report — showing the emotional and historical relationship between their life and their ancestor's life. When their grandmother was exactly their age, it was 1951. Rationing had just ended. That was her present moment.

That feeling prompts them to act. They create a permanent page for their ancestor — a name, a photograph, a memory, a URL that will still exist in a hundred years.

The tool creates the emotion. The emotion builds the archive.

The public value

Still Here is building something no institution currently has: a distributed, human-scale archive of ordinary Scottish lives, created by the families who lived them.

Not a genealogy database. Not a memorial service. Something warmer and more permanent than either — a record of how it actually felt to be alive in Scotland across the last two centuries, told in the voices of the people who were there.

The platform is free to use. It always will be.

Why now

Still Here launches in 2026. Phase 1 — the Same Sky tool — is in active development. Phase 2 opens the permanent ancestor record archive. Phase 3 introduces living testimony tools designed specifically for use in palliative care and community settings.

By Phase 4 the platform will carry a growing archive of Scottish family records, open to institutional partnership, research access, and civic co-programming.

The ask

We are not seeking funding at this stage. We are seeking one of three things:

A named archive partner — willing to receive and preserve Still Here records in perpetuity if the platform ever changes. This is the single assurance that makes the platform trustworthy to the families who use it.

A palliative care or hospice partner — willing to pilot the living testimony tools ahead of Phase 3 launch and help shape them for clinical and community settings.

A cultural or diaspora partner — willing to co-present the platform at its civic launch and help connect it to the communities it was built for.

If Still Here sounds like something your organisation should be part of, we would welcome a conversation.
